Taiwan used to be the most underrated destination in Asia. When you ask people where they want to go in this part of the world, you would hear, say, Thailand or Japan most often. But ever since the ROC-Taiwan launched a visa-free initiative for Filipinos last November, this small but terrific country took on social media by storm with witty travel hashtags to boot. It's only a 9-month trial period to last until end of July 2018 but we're hoping it sticks for good.

We went to Taiwan in the late autumn of 2016 (first week of December). Even though it was not a popular country to visit back then, it held a special place in my heart already. My grandpa came from the mainland but when I was young, Taiwan is what I remember to be the place he went to and fro, and as a kid, I was always excited for his return. Well, he had been gone for many years now but I still feel like wandering around his second home is a way to connect to him, just in spirit, you know?

We chose stay in the Beitou area of Taipei because it is famous for its hot spring waters. In the morning, we'd soak up the nature, climbing uphill and stopping at the various facilities on the road. It's possible to take a car up but walking under the shade of trees felt much more zen.

Afterward, we would take on the rest of the city hassle-free thru the Taipei Metro, one of the most efficient train systems in Asia, if not the world. We spent our afternoons hunting down the best xiao long bao and milk tea, hopping from resto to resto. Then, we tried to get some culture amidst our gluttony and spent time in places like the Chiang Kai Shek Memorial Hall. The symmetry of the structures were awe-inspiring. At night, we went to night markets for glorious food. Our favorite is Shilin Night Market, where you could spend hours just shopping and eating. I'm drooling just thinking about the Torched Beef with Cumin Pepper. Yum.

On the next day, we decided to go into the heart of the country. We took the train to Taichung where we stayed at this lovely Airbnb with kind hosts who provided us detailed instructions on how to go up the mountain area. There's a spot called Fengchia night market worth checking out before you leave this city. The Salted Sweet Potato Fries are bomb AF. From Taichung, you only need to take 1 bus to go up Cingjing.

Check out the places we stayed at.

Upon entering Cingjing Farm, we almost went kid-crazy chasing down the free-roaming sheep to feed them with grass pellets. All you will see in this farm are blues, greens and these beige fluffies. You can feed them with your hand, you see. I would say this was the highlight of our trip and we'll come back for more. On the next blog post, you will read about Sun Moon Lake and Alishan Tea.
